Healthy Harbour Communities

Harbour areas play an important role in regional economies, providing employment and supporting international trade. However, neighbourhoods located near harbour infrastructure often face unique environmental and social challenges. Residents may experience increased noise, traffic congestion and limited access to green spaces.

At the same time, harbour communities frequently possess strong social networks and a distinct local identity. Understanding how these factors interact is important for improving quality of life in these areas.

The Healthy Harbour Communities project investigates how the physical environment of harbour neighbourhoods influences the health and well-being of residents. The research focuses on several districts in Vlissingen and Terneuzen where harbour activities are located close to residential areas.

Researchers collect both quantitative and qualitative data. Spatial analysis is used to examine factors such as the availability of green spaces, accessibility of public facilities and walkability of neighbourhood streets. These indicators are combined with demographic data and regional health statistics.

To complement the spatial analysis, the project team organizes interviews and focus groups with local residents. Participants discuss their experiences of living near harbour infrastructure and identify factors that affect their daily well-being.

Workshops are also organized with local organizations, including municipal planners, public health professionals and community groups. These workshops allow stakeholders to explore possible interventions such as improving public spaces, enhancing mobility options or creating community meeting areas.

A central aim of the project is to develop practical tools that municipalities can use when designing healthier neighbourhoods. The research team therefore translates findings into guidelines for urban planning and community engagement.

By combining spatial research with community participation, the project seeks to provide insights that help harbour cities create living environments that support both economic activity and public well-being.
