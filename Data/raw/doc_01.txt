Collected  proof: 
When I started this year, I was used to the amount of work from the secondary education. I thought it would be the same, 
but o was I wrong. I had a lot of problems trying to keep up with the problem-solving sessions we had after classes. I was 
thinking I could do everything on my own to show that I am capable of doing things. I was scared to ask questions and this 
lead to insecurity . This lack of structure and self-consciousness didn’t help me at all. I noticed I needed to do something 
about it. 
 
I took action by incorporating short breaks into my problem-solving sessions. These breaks provided me with the 
opportunity to recharge and gain a fresh perspective. Additionally, I began reflecting on past experiences to refine my 
problem-solving methods. By analyzing successes and failures, I could adapt and improve my approach. 
 
Later on, I noticed a significant improvement in my problem-solving abilities as I became more comfortable seeking 
assistance. Peers and instructors provided positive feedback on my collaborative efforts, reinforcing the value of 
teamwork. 
 
Moving forward, I understand the importance of offering constructive feedback and recognition for collaborative 
endeavors. This fosters a supportive environment that encourages everyone to contribute their best. I now realize that 
continuous reflection and adaptation are essential for personal growth.

Planning & Monitoring ahead 
 
Student check:  
 
Collected proof: 
My starting point was realizing my planning issue when feedback highlighted my frequent forgetfulness. Despite being 
aware, I hadn't actively addressed it until PPD encouraged me to take action. 
 
I began using the mobile app reminders, which notifies me whenever I set a reminder. This significantly reduced my 
forgetfulness by keeping track of important tasks. Additionally, I incorporated the use of a whiteboard in my room for 
weekly planning. Every Sunday, I outline my tasks for the week ahead, ensuring I stay organized Furthermore, I 
synchronized my Outlook calendar with my school calendar to consolidate all my appointments in one place. This was 
crucial since I tend to check only one source, often overlooking notes on other activities . 
 
Later on, these methods proved effective, not only in enhancing my organization but also in relieving stress and 
significantly reducing my forgetfulness. And not to forget my family and friends really appreciated that I stopped 
forgetting so much. 
 
Moving forward, I will continue to employ these strategies. Now recognizing their effectiveness, I see a clear path toward a 
more organized life and happy people around me, which, in turn, minimizes stress and improves my memory.

Motivation and providing support 
 
Student check:  
 
Collected proof: 
When I started this course, I had a lot of initial motivation, primarily driven by my dream to eventually work at my father's 
place. However, I found myself lacking motivation in certain areas, particularly in game development, where I struggled to 
find intrinsic drive. 
 
To address this lack of motivation, I found that circumstances often pushed me to work despite my initial reluctance. 
When faced with tasks I didn't enjoy, I reminded myself of my overarching goals: to pass the year cum-laude and obtain 
my propaedeutic certificate. These goals were significant to me as they represented both personal achievement and the 
desire to make my family proud. I sometimes think I set my own goals too high, but then I think that it is okay to set high 
goals, as long as there are more reachable goals in between. This helps me having a good feeling of work I do, eventough I 
don’t reach my high stretched goals. 
 
Despite challenges, my determination to achieve these goals kept me motivated, even when working on projects I didn't 
particularly enjoy. For instance, in a group project on Object-Oriented Programming (OOP), I faced difficulties when my 
teammates lacked motivation. I contemplated whether to take on all tasks myself or encourage my teammates to 
contribute, ultimately opting for the latter as I prioritized achieving good grades. 
 
Moving forward, I recognize that not everyone shares the same level of motivation, and I need to adapt accordingly. While 
I continue to work towards my propaedeutic certificate, achieving cum-laude requires further effort. With a current 
average of 7.6, I understand the need to work harder, particularly in areas where I struggle. I believe that with dedication 
and collaboration in future projects, I can improve my grades and continue striving towards my goals.

Methodical approach   
 
Student check:   
 
Collected proof: 
 
When I started this year, my approach to task management was rather unstructured. I often completed tasks as they came 
to mind, without considering their priority or logical sequence. This is something I took with from the secondary school,  I 
just did what needed to be done in a un-logical order and without structure. 
 
To improve, I began using task management tools such as Trello and Notion. I started by listing all tasks related to my 
projects and then organizing them based on priority and deadlines. For example, in the OOP project, I created a Trello 
board with columns for “To Do,” “In Progress,” and “Done.” I further prioritized tasks within each column by due date and 
importance. I did the same for the FPR project, I made a notion page where I structure my tasks my data and what needs 
to be done first. 
 
Moving forward, I know I have to continue refining my prioritization skills by experimenting with different tools and 
methods, such as the Eisenhower Matrix, to further enhance my efficiency and effectiveness in task management. 
 
 
See appendix 11: Trello board for OOP, Notion page with deadlines and priority, Eisenhower matrix in my notion page.
